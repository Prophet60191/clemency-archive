# Project Communications Plan

**Purpose:** To ensure consistent, professional, and effective communication with all project stakeholders, including volunteers, funders, and the general public.

---

## 1. Audience & Key Messages

| Audience | Key Messages | Primary Channel |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Volunteers** | Your work is valuable; Here is how to get help; Here is our progress. | Slack, Email | 
| **Funders** | We are responsible stewards of your investment; We are meeting our milestones; Here is our impact. | Email, Formal Reports |
| **General Public** | This history is important; Here is what we are discovering; Here is how you can use our data. | Website/Blog, Social Media |
| **Academic/Genealogy Community** | Our data is robust and well-documented; Here is how to cite our work; We welcome collaboration. | Academic Journals, Conferences, Website |

---

## 2. Communication Channels & Purpose

*   **Slack Workspace:**
    *   **Purpose:** Day-to-day internal communication, volunteer questions, team-building, and informal updates. This is our virtual office.
    *   **Lead:** Project Leader / Team Leads.

*   **Email Newsletter:**
    *   **Purpose:** Formal, scheduled updates for a broad audience (volunteers and interested public). Used for major announcements and progress summaries.
    *   **Lead:** Project Leader.

*   **Project Website/Blog:**
    *   **Purpose:** The public face of the project. A home for static information ('About', 'Data'), long-form stories discovered in the data, and detailed methodology.
    *   **Lead:** Project Leader.

*   **Social Media (e.g., Twitter, Mastodon):**
    *   **Purpose:** Sharing quick findings, promoting blog posts, engaging with the wider historical and genealogy communities, and volunteer recruitment.
    *   **Lead:** [To Be Assigned]
