The project aims to digitize and catalog New York State Executive Clemency information from the state archives, making it accessible and searchable for researchers, advocates, policymakers, and the public. The initiative seeks to address the lack of comprehensive and accessible data on executive clemency in New York State and provide valuable insights into sentencing trends, policy and political shifts, and the impact of clemency decisions.

## Background and Rationale:
Executive clemency, including pardons, commutations, reprieves, and restoration of rights, plays a significant role in the criminal justice system, yet access to information about clemency decisions is limited.

Currently, clemency records are not readily accessible beyond Freedom of Information Law (FOIL) requests, hindering research and advocacy efforts related to clemency and criminal justice reform.

By digitizing and cataloging clemency records, the project aims to fill this gap and provide researchers and advocates with comprehensive and searchable data on clemency decisions in New York State.

## Objectives:

*   Digitize and catalog New York State Executive Clemency records from the state archives.
*   Create a searchable database accessible to researchers, advocates, policymakers, and the public.
*   Analyze historical trends and patterns in clemency decisions to inform research, advocacy, and policy efforts.
*   Promote transparency, accountability, and public awareness of executive clemency processes and outcomes.

## Key Activities:

*   Procure copies (print or digital) of clemency records from the state archives and any previously digitized scans.
*   Digitize and transcribe clemency records into a searchable database format.
*   Develop an online platform to host the database, providing user-friendly search and filtering functionalities.
*   Engage stakeholders, including researchers, advocates, policymakers, and directly impacted individuals, to gather input and feedback on database features and usability.
*   Conduct training sessions and workshops to educate users on how to access and utilize the database effectively.
*   Monitor usage and feedback to continuously improve the database and ensure its relevance and usefulness to stakeholders.

## Expected Outcomes:

*   A comprehensive and searchable database of New York State Executive Clemency records, spanning historical data from the 1700s to the present.
*   Increased accessibility to clemency information for researchers, advocates, policymakers, and the public, facilitating data-driven research and advocacy efforts.
*   Insights into historical trends and patterns in clemency decisions, shedding light on shifts in sentencing practices, political dynamics, and the impact of clemency policies over time.
*   Enhanced transparency, accountability, and public awareness of executive clemency processes and outcomes, promoting informed discourse and policy discussions on criminal justice reform.

## Sustainability Plan:

*   Diversify funding sources beyond the initial grant period, including additional grants, partnerships, and income-generating activities.
*   Foster partnerships and collaborations with relevant organizations and stakeholders to leverage resources, expertise, and networks.
*   Engage with and seek support from communities and stakeholders to build ownership and sustainability of the project.

## Monitoring and Evaluation:

## Project Summary:
The primary goal of our project is to digitally catalog all New York State executive clemency information currently housed within the state archive. By doing so, we aim to make this information accessible and searchable, primarily for individuals conducting research on clemency in New York. This initiative will facilitate comprehensive data analysis and enable researchers to identify trends and patterns related to executive clemency in the state.

## Target Population:
The primary target population for our project includes researchers and scholars interested in clemency and the historical trends of executive clemency in New York State. This encompasses individuals from academic institutions, research organizations, legal advocacy groups, and other entities focused on criminal justice reform and historical analysis.

## Problem Statement:
Currently, accessing data related to executive clemency in New York State is restricted to Freedom of Information Law (FOIL) requests, presenting a significant barrier to researchers and scholars interested in studying historical trends and patterns. Our project aims to address this problem by digitizing and cataloging executive clemency information from the state archive, making it accessible beyond FOIL requests. By providing researchers with comprehensive data on clemency applications, grants, and decisions—including commutations, pardons, reprieves, and restoration of rights—we seek to enable in-depth analysis of how these decisions correlate with historical shifts in sentencing practices, political landscapes, and other contextual factors.

## Project Activities:

*   **Digitization of Ledgers:** The project will commence with the scanning of all relevant ledgers containing executive clemency information from the New York State Archive. This step will involve utilizing high-quality scanning equipment to create digital copies of the ledgers, ensuring the preservation and accessibility of the historical data.
*   **Data Entry into Spreadsheet:** Following the digitization process, the next step will involve meticulously transcribing the clemency data from the scanned images into a structured spreadsheet format. This comprehensive spreadsheet will capture key details, including applicant information, clemency types (commutations, pardons, reprieves, restoration of rights), decision outcomes, dates, and any additional relevant data points.

## Evaluation Plan:
The success of our project will be measured by achieving two primary objectives. Firstly, we aim to have all executive clemency data digitized and accessible in a searchable format, allowing for various filtering options to enhance usability and research capabilities. Secondly, we will evaluate the effectiveness of our data entry processes by ensuring that the digitized data is close to 100% accurate, thus enhancing its reliability for research and analysis purposes.

## Partnerships and Collaborations:
At present, our project does not have any existing partnerships or collaborations. However, we recognize the potential value of collaborating with relevant organizations, such as academic institutions, research centers, or advocacy groups focused on criminal justice reform and historical analysis. These partnerships could provide expertise, resources, or additional support to enhance the implementation and impact of our project.

## Timeline for Project Implementation:
Our project will be implemented in several phases to ensure the successful digitization and accessibility of executive clemency data from the New York State Archive. While the specific timeline may be subject to adjustment based on various factors, we have outlined a preliminary timeline based on our current understanding of the project scope and requirements.

### Phase 1: Preliminary Planning (1 month)

*   Conduct initial research and planning to determine project scope, requirements, and resource needs.
*   Identify key stakeholders and potential partners for collaboration.
*   Develop a detailed project plan, including timeline, budget, and deliverables.

### Phase 2: Digitization of Ledgers (3 months)

*   Procure necessary equipment and resources for digitization.
*   Commence scanning of all ledgers containing executive clemency information from the New York State Archive.
*   Monitor progress and address any technical issues or challenges as they arise.

### Phase 3: Data Entry and Quality Control (6 months)

*   Begin transcription of digitized clemency data into a structured spreadsheet format.
*   Implement quality control measures to ensure the accuracy and reliability of the entered data.
*   Conduct regular reviews and audits of the data entry process to identify and address any discrepancies or errors.

### Phase 4: Database Development and Testing (2 months)

*   Develop a searchable database platform for storing and accessing the digitized clemency data.
*   Test the functionality and usability of the database, incorporating feedback from stakeholders as needed.
*   Fine-tune the database interface and search features to optimize user experience.

### Phase 5: Launch and Evaluation (1 month)

*   Officially launch the searchable database platform to the public and relevant stakeholders.
*   Monitor usage and gather feedback from users to evaluate the effectiveness and usability of the database.
*   Make any necessary adjustments or improvements based on user feedback and usage data.

## Resources and Support Needs:

*   **Funding for Scanning Services:** We require funding to purchase copies of the executive clemency documents from the New York State archive and cover any costs associated with accessing previously digitized scans. While the archive will handle the scanning process, financial support is needed to acquire the necessary copies and digitized scans for our project.
*   **Database Program:** We need access to an adequate database program capable of efficiently storing, organizing, and retrieving the digitized clemency data. The database program should support versatile filtering options to facilitate research and analysis by end-users.
*   **Storage Capacity:** Adequate storage capacity is essential to hold the digitized clemency data securely and ensure accessibility over the long term. This includes both physical storage solutions and digital storage space for the database.
*   **Volunteers for Data Digitization:** We require a team of approximately 5-10 volunteers with strong attention to detail and data entry skills to assist with transcribing the digitized clemency data into a structured spreadsheet format.
*   **Training and Supervision:** Providing training and ongoing supervision for volunteers to ensure consistency and accuracy in data digitization processes.
*   **Quality Assurance:** Implementing quality assurance measures to verify the accuracy and reliability of the digitized data, such as double-entry verification and regular quality checks.
*   **Technical Support:** Access to technical support services to address any issues or challenges that may arise during the digitization and database development process.

*   **Promotion and Outreach:** We will implement targeted promotion and outreach efforts to raise awareness of the database and expand its user base. This includes outreach to relevant academic institutions, legal organizations, advocacy groups, and other potential users, as well as leveraging social media, conferences, and other platforms to showcase the database's capabilities and benefits.
*   **Long-Term Sustainability Plan:** Our organization has developed a long-term sustainability plan for maintaining and supporting the database beyond the grant funding period. This plan includes exploring alternative funding sources, establishing partnerships or collaborations, and exploring revenue-generating opportunities to sustain the project's operations and growth over time.

## Unique Aspects and Innovative Approaches:
One of the distinctive features of our project is the comprehensive and exhaustive nature of the digitized executive clemency data it will provide. Upon completion, our database will contain a wealth of information on executive clemency decisions in New York State, spanning from historical records to the present day. This level of detail and comprehensiveness sets our project apart from others addressing similar issues.

Furthermore, it will be the sole entity to possess such a comprehensive database. Not even the state of New York has readily available data. This unique aspect underscores the significance and value of our project in filling a critical gap in access to information for researchers, advocates, and stakeholders in the criminal justice field.

By providing unprecedented access to this wealth of historical and contemporary data, our project empowers researchers, policymakers, and advocates with the tools they need to conduct in-depth analyses, track trends, and inform evidence-based policy recommendations. This innovative approach to data accessibility and transparency positions our project as a trailblazer in the field, poised to make a lasting impact on efforts to advance criminal justice reform in New York State and beyond.
